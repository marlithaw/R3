01
Reflection
The objective examination of what is actually happening
R
Reflect
What · entry point
Metacognition
Thinking about thinking — the capacity to observe your own mental process without being consumed by it.Flavell (1979); Brown (1987)
Mindfulness
Present-moment, non-judgmental observation as the precondition for any conscious response.Kabat-Zinn (1990)
Cognitive defusion
Stepping back from thoughts and emotions to observe them rather than being fused with them.Hayes, ACT (1999)
A
Aware & Attentive
How · two points of analysis
Affect labeling
Naming an emotion reduces amygdala activity — the act of labeling interrupts automatic escalation.Lieberman et al. (2007)
Interoception
The body's internal signaling system — reading physiological cues before the cognitive mind catches up.Craig (2009); Porges
Somatic experiencing
Trauma is stored in the body and must be read through physical sensation before cognitive processing can occur.Levine (1997); van der Kolk
P
Purposeful Pondering
Why · forward lens
Cognitive reappraisal
Reinterpreting the meaning of a situation — one of the most effective regulation strategies, reduces emotional intensity without suppression.Gross (1998)
Solution-focused thinking
Forward-oriented inquiry activates prefrontal cortex problem-solving rather than limbic threat response.de Shazer (1985)
02
Resistance
The intentional interruption of automatic response patterns
R
Resist
What · the interruption
Inhibitory control
The executive function that allows suppression of dominant automatic responses in favor of goal-directed ones.Diamond (2013)
Pause-and-plan response
Counterpart to fight-or-flight — the nervous system's capacity to slow, evaluate, and select response under stress.Taylor et al. (2000)
Habit interruption
Automatic behaviors run on cue-routine-reward loops. Interruption at the cue stage is the most effective intervention point.Duhigg (2012)
A
Act
How · three-layer check
Pattern recognition
Distinguishing conditioned from conscious response activates prefrontal evaluation over limbic automaticity.Siegel (2010)
Values clarification
Values-congruent behavior produces sustained motivation and reduced emotional distress.Hayes, ACT (1999)
Consequential thinking
Anticipating outcomes activates future-self projection — a prefrontal function that counteracts impulsive limbic response.Hershfield (2011)
P
Purposeful Pressing or Pivoting
Why · conscious direction
Self-determination theory
Autonomy — choosing direction from internal values rather than external pressure — is a core driver of sustained behavioral change.Deci & Ryan (1985)
Motivational interviewing
Ambivalence between pressing and pivoting is resolved through values-aligned decision making, not willpower.Miller & Rollnick (1991)
03
Revelation
The emergence of truth that Reflection and Resistance together produce
R
Reveal
What · generative output
Insight research
Sudden insight produces a distinct neural signature — gamma wave burst in the right anterior temporal lobe — different from incremental problem solving.Jung-Beeman et al. (2004)
Post-traumatic growth
Meaning-making from difficult experience produces measurable positive change in self-perception, relationships, and life philosophy.Tedeschi & Calhoun (1996)
A
Accept
How · receive and integrate
Self-compassion
Receiving truth about oneself without shame or deflection requires non-judgment — which is not passive, it is regulatory.Neff (2003)
Narrative integration
Integrating new understanding into existing self-concept is the mechanism by which insight produces lasting change rather than temporary relief.Siegel (2010)
Expressive writing
Translating emotional experience into language produces measurable improvements in psychological and physical health.Pennebaker (1997)
P
Purposeful Practicing
Why · compounding growth
Neuroplasticity
Neurons that fire together wire together — repeated conscious choice literally rewires neural pathways, replacing automatic responses with new defaults.Hebb (1949); Doidge (2007)
Deliberate practice
Measurable, repeatable, feedback-informed practice is the mechanism of lasting skill acquisition — not insight alone.Ericsson et al. (1993)
Habit formation
New behavioral patterns require consistent repetition to become automatic — practice is not optional, it is the mechanism.Lally et al. (2010)

R³ is a sequenced process where the science of each step informs why it must come before the next. Reflection activates metacognition and somatic awareness. Resistance applies inhibitory control and values alignment. Revelation produces insight, integration, and — through Purposeful Practicing — neuroplastic change. The sequence is the intervention.
